About Myers Briggs Personality Prediction

Welcome to the Myers Briggs Personality Prediction platform, a project presented by Thayananth A/L Kumaresan and supervised by Dr. Vimala Balakrishnan.

Introduction to Personality Traits

Personality traits refer to the unique tendencies individuals exhibit in their behavior, thoughts, or feelings (Ashton, 2013). Measuring personality involves using self-report questionnaires like the Myers–Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI) and others, each with its own set of strengths and limitations.

Introduction to Myers–Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI)

The MBTI, utilized by over 2 million test takers in 115 countries and available in 29 languages, is a widely recognized personality inventory. It originated from the work of Katherine Briggs and her daughter, building on Carl Jung's theory of psychological types. This tool is frequently employed in business, counseling, and education, demonstrating high reliability and validity (Tzeng et al., 1989; McCaulley & Moody, 2008).

Description of the Dataset

The project utilizes the Myers-Briggs Personality (MBTI) Type Dataset obtained from Kaggle, consisting of 8600 rows with 4-letter personality type labels and text from the last 50 posts made by users. The dataset, chosen for its size, diversity, and availability, plays a crucial role in the research objectives.

Methodology

The methodology involves data cleaning, exploratory data analysis, feature engineering, and ensemble modeling using machine learning techniques such as Logistic Regression, Light Gradient Boosting, and others. Feature extraction includes traditional text representation, and the dataset is evaluated using various metrics.

Deployment

The culmination of this project will be a web application, providing users with an interactive and insightful experience in exploring personality traits through text analysis.
