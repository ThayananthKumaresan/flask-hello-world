Introduction to Personality Traits

Personality traits refer to the unique tendencies individuals exhibit in their behavior, thoughts, or feelings (Ashton, 2013). Measuring personality involves using self-report questionnaires like the Myers–Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI) and others, each with its own set of strengths and limitations.

Introduction to Myers–Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI)

First, let's go into Myers-Briggs personality types. Myers and Briggs were 2 intelligent women in the early 1900s who noticed different people thought in different ways. They decided to analyse and classify the different people around them. During WWII, their published research and personality types were used to identify the best job type for women to do while the men were off at war.


Currently the MBTI test is utilized by over 2 million test takers in 115 countries and available in 29 languages, is a widely recognized personality inventory. It originated from the work of Katherine Briggs and her daughter, building on Carl Jung's theory of psychological types. This tool is frequently employed in business, counseling, and education, demonstrating high reliability and validity (Tzeng et al., 1989; McCaulley & Moody, 2008).
